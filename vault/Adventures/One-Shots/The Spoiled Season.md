# The Spoiled Season

> [!info] Overview
> **Hook:** Someone hired the PCs to find out why Rackby's fish cure is failing, or they stumble into a settlement in the middle of a very bad season.
> **Dramatic question:** Will the PCs expose who knew the salt was degrading, and what will they do with that knowledge?
> **Tone:** Low-stakes in blood, high-stakes in livelihoods. An accounting problem with consequences people can't afford to ignore.
> **Locations:** [[Rackby]], [[The Fork]], (optional) [[Caltworks]]
> **Key NPCs:** [[Vess Cordham]], Tamma Wreich, Tallin Oss, Burl, [[Dessa Vael]]

## The Situation

It is late summer. Rackby is three weeks into the curing season. The fish are running well. The problem is the salt.

Three families have lost barrels to spoilage already. Another four are watching their packed vats with rising worry. The smell from the curing sheds is wrong, a sweetish rot underneath the brine rather than clean, sharp preservation. Everyone doing the work knows what that smell means.

The boat owners are arguing about whose fault it is. The curers blame the salt. The permanent owners point at handling errors. Nobody wants to say what they suspect, because what they suspect implicates the one person who holds both ends of their supply chain.

[[Vess Cordham]] has been managing the salt arrangement for eleven years. He has known for two seasons that the Caltworks product is degrading. He has said nothing. He is now four months into building a replacement supplier at [[Silthen]] and expects to complete the transition quietly, framing it as an upgrade rather than an emergency response.

This season is the season that plan runs out of room.

**What Cordham knows and no one else does:**
- The Caltworks salt has been declining for two seasons. Quality is now bad enough to cause consistent spoilage.
- He has arranged a secondary supplier at [[Silthen]] through [[Hava Denn]], but the volume is not yet large enough to replace Caltworks.
- He approached [[Dessa Vael]] through an intermediary about timing a hauler stoppage at [[Caltworks]] to disrupt a Calt family shipment, which would create cover to shift supply sources.
- His dock manager, Burl, knows about the Silthen arrangement. Burl has debts being called in by someone outside Rackby.

**What the PCs can discover, if they look:**
- Tallin Oss at [[The Fork]] keeps crate logs that show the real quantity and dates of salt leaving Caltworks, which do not match what Cordham has declared to buyers.
- Cedd Miralle at [[The Fork]] has already noticed the discrepancy and is sitting on it.
- Burl will talk if his debt pressure reaches the right level.
- [[Dessa Vael]] does not know Cordham is behind the approach, but she can describe the intermediary.

> [!warning]- GM Only: What Actually Happened
> Cordham is not a thief and has not defrauded anyone in a technical sense. He bought salt at the price he could get, delivered it, and charged accordingly. What he did not do is tell Rackby that the product was declining while continuing to charge full-quality rates. He also withheld the existence of a backup option, timing its disclosure to benefit his own position.
>
> The curers are right. The salt is bad. The spoilage is entirely a salt quality problem, not a handling error.
>
> If the PCs find this out and bring it to Tamma Wreich, she will want answers from Cordham. Cordham will offer explanations. Some are true. The settlement will need to decide what comes next, and the players are the ones who decide how much pressure they put on the situation.

## Act 1: The Smell Is Wrong

**Dramatic question:** Will the PCs identify the salt as the source of spoilage before Cordham redirects the investigation toward the curers?

### Opening

The PCs arrive at Rackby. Choose the entry that fits:

- **Hired:** Tamma Wreich sent a message to a nearby settlement requesting someone to assess a spoilage problem. She is not sure if it is fraud, error, or bad luck and wants someone who does not work for Cordham.
- **Passing through:** They are heading somewhere else. The smell at the gut tables is distinctive and one of the permanent residents asks them to look at something.
- **Following a rumor:** Word in the last settlement they were in: a fish factor near the lake is offering double rate for carters willing to move product fast. Someone is in a hurry.

### What the PCs Find

The Landing is tense. Gut tables are running, boats are coming in, but the workers are moving with the particular economy of people who are not sure the effort is worth it. Three barrel stacks near the south smokehouse are roped off with rag markers, the spoiled stock.

**Tamma Wreich** is at the Landing when the PCs arrive, or will find them quickly. She is direct:

> "We lost close to two hundred barrels last year. We are on track to lose more this year and the season is not half over. Three families are looking at a year of loss. I want to know where the fault lies before I sit down with Cordham and let him explain it to me."

She is willing to pay for a real answer, not a comfortable one.

**What PCs can investigate here:**
- The spoiled barrels. A good look at the rotten product reveals the brine is thin and inconsistent. The salt dissolved unevenly. This is a salt quality problem.
- The curers. They will say immediately that the salt this season is different, lighter, less crystalline, and that they raised it with Burl when the shipment came in. Burl said it was within tolerance.
- Burl. Cordham's dock manager knows the salt quality is off because the Silthen grey salt Cordham is blending in is lower grade than what Caltworks used to produce. He will not say this. He will say tolerance is tolerance and the curers need to adjust their ratios. He is not comfortable and the PCs can tell if they press him.
- The Factor's Store. The loading dock has inventory ledgers. A successful look at them reveals that this season's salt shipment was logged as "mixed batch, Caltworks-adjacent" rather than Caltworks direct, a different entry than all prior seasons.

> [!tip] GM Craft: Burl
> Burl is loyal to Cordham, not because Cordham is good to him, but because Cordham gave him a job when he needed one and has never made it conditional. But Burl has debts. Someone outside Rackby has been sending letters. The PCs can find this out by asking around the Bed Floor. Burl's room is at the end, and he has been keeping odd hours.
>
> Burl will not flip for money. He will flip if the PCs make a credible case that his debts are about to become Cordham's problem, which he does not want. Or if they offer him somewhere to go that is not here.

**Complication:** Cordham himself is in Rackby. He is smooth, prepared, and has a version of events ready. He will invite the PCs for a drink. His explanation will be calibrated: yes, the quality has been variable, he has been looking into it, a better arrangement may be coming. He will not lie, but he will not disclose. He will offer to help the investigation if asked, and his help will steer it toward [[Caltworks]] and away from [[The Fork]].

But the "Caltworks-adjacent" entry in his ledger is already in the PCs' hands, or could be.

## Act 2: What The Logs Say

**Dramatic question:** Will the PCs obtain the evidence at [[The Fork]] before Cordham realizes what they are looking for and applies pressure to Oss?

### The Road

[[The Fork]] is a day and a half by cart from Rackby. On the road, the PCs may encounter:

- A Cordham carter heading the other way, who mentions that the factor has sent a message ahead to Tallin Oss. He does not know what it said. He says this casually, as information, not as a warning.
- A hauler from [[Caltworks]] moving a half-load, delayed by something upstream. She mentions the weighmaster at Caltworks has been keeping shipments back, running checks before release. Something about declared weight.

### At The Fork

The Fork is quiet in the way that places are quiet when everyone knows each other's business and no one says anything. Praven Holdt is at the animal yard. The rest house has three other travelers.

**Tallin Oss** is the one the PCs need. He is precise, careful, and has been running the storage logs for eight seasons. He knows what Cordham's message said, because Oss always reads messages before deciding whether to act on them. The message said: "Hold all logs pending a routine factor audit. Do not release copies."

Oss is not going to break his working arrangement with Cordham over a stranger's inquiry. But he is also not going to falsify his logs, and he is not comfortable with what the logs show.

**What the logs contain:**
- The last three Caltworks salt shipments declared for Rackby were logged through The Fork at weights 12-18% below the declared amounts on Cordham's Rackby receipts. Either Caltworks is short-shipping, Cordham is adjusting the records, or product is leaving through the northeast branch.
- Two small loads in the last four months left The Fork via the northeast road. They were not logged as salt. Oss noted them without categorizing them. If asked, he admits they smelled of brine and were moving toward the Silthen estuary direction.

**Cedd Miralle** will approach the PCs once he knows why they are there. He has wanted someone outside The Fork to look at these numbers for months. He will share his comparison of the logs against Caltworks' declared output, which he obtained through a contact at the weighmaster's house. He does not have all the pieces but he has enough.

> [!warning]- GM Only: What Miralle Wants
> Miralle is not purely public-spirited. He had a transaction blocked by [[Maren Seedeck]] three years ago and has never gotten a clear reason. He suspects the Calt family used her to freeze him out. He wants the Calt family's numbers examined because he thinks it will show they are defrauding both buyers and sellers. He may be right, but his reasons color his interpretation. If the PCs use him as a source, Cordham can credibly characterize him as someone with an axe to grind.

**Complication:** Cordham arrives at The Fork by the time the PCs have had a productive conversation with Oss. He is alone, traveling fast. He is not threatening. He asks what the PCs are trying to find out, and he says he may be able to help them find it faster.

If the PCs have the log discrepancy evidence, this is where Cordham decides whether to explain or deflect. He will try to explain first.

His explanation, if given: "The Caltworks batches have been underperforming for two seasons. I have been sourcing supplementary product from the west. The mixed batches at the landing were an error of judgment on my part. I should have disclosed it. I am working on a better arrangement."

This is true. It is also not all of it.

## Act 3: The Accounting

**Dramatic question:** Will the PCs give Tamma Wreich what she needs to make a decision, and what will that decision cost?

### The Confrontation (or Negotiation)

The PCs return to Rackby with whatever they have. Tamma Wreich calls a meeting at the Factor's Store, with Cordham present. What happens depends on what the PCs bring and how they play it.

The situation on the table:
- The Caltworks salt has been degrading for two seasons. Cordham knew. He did not tell Rackby.
- He has been blending in grey salt from [[Silthen]] without disclosure, which is lower quality than Caltworks product.
- The Silthen arrangement is real and has been running for four months.
- He has been charging Caltworks prices for mixed or declining product.

**What Cordham will offer, if pressured:**
- A reduced rate retroactively for the last two seasons, enough to compensate the spoilage losses.
- Full transition to the Silthen supplier within two seasons, at disclosed pricing.
- A seat at the table for Tamma Wreich in the next contract negotiation.

**What Cordham will not offer without a fight:**
- Admitting he timed the transition to protect himself rather than Rackby.
- Explaining the Dessa Vael approach (he will deny it unless presented with the intermediary's name).
- Losing his exclusive arrangement as the sole factor for the Rackby salt supply.

**What Tamma Wreich wants:**
- Compensation for the three worst-hit families.
- Price transparency going forward, or the right to negotiate directly with suppliers.
- An answer to whether this was deliberate fraud or bad judgment. She can live with bad judgment. She is less sure about the other.

| Outcome | How It Happens | Consequences |
|---|---|---|
| Cordham makes good | PCs present evidence, Wreich negotiates, Cordham agrees to terms | Spoilage families compensated, Cordham survives damaged. Silthen transition happens on Rackby's terms. |
| Cordham loses the arrangement | PCs expose the Dessa Vael scheme, or Burl talks, or both | Rackby cuts the exclusive. Cordham has to renegotiate from scratch. Silthen still happens. |
| Rackby goes direct | PCs connect Wreich to [[Maren Seedeck]] at [[Caltworks]] | Direct relationship established. Cordham loses the salt side. He still controls fish export. Settlement may be worse. |
| Nothing formal changes | PCs let Cordham buy them off or give up | Silthen transition happens anyway. Rackby does not know for another season. Next season's spoilage is someone else's problem. |
| Dessa Vael surfaces | PCs trace the intermediary back to Cordham | Cordham is accused of deliberately disrupting Caltworks supply for his own gain. The settlement has to decide whether to prosecute that. There is no law here, only consensus and consequence. |

> [!warning]- GM Only: What The Players Won't Know
> There is no formal law in Rackby or Caltworks. Accountability is social. If Cordham is exposed and the settlements agree he acted wrongly, he loses relationships and income. That is the maximum sanction. The PCs should feel the weight of this: the system that is supposed to correct what Cordham did is the same informal trust network he built over eleven years. He is being judged by people who depend on him.
>
> The Silthen arrangement, if disclosed properly, is actually good for Rackby. [[Hava Denn]] is a fair counterpart. The grey salt is lower grade but honest about it. The problem is not the solution Cordham found. The problem is how he got there.

---

## Aftermath

Whatever the PCs do, the salt corridor changes:

- **If Cordham survives with terms:** He completes the Silthen transition under scrutiny. He is less trusted, more careful, and for the first time in a decade has someone checking his figures.
- **If Cordham is removed from the salt side:** Rackby has to build a direct relationship with either Caltworks or Silthen. This is harder than it sounds. Cordham built that infrastructure. Replacing it takes seasons.
- **If nothing is resolved:** The next bad cure season, which is coming, happens with no accountability in place. The families who lost product this season make less than nothing. Some of them leave.
- **If Dessa Vael's involvement surfaces:** The Caltworks hauler situation becomes a connected thread. The players have now touched two crises, not one. The coordinator at Caltworks is watching what happens.

The fish are going to spoil or not spoil regardless of what the PCs do. The question is who knows why, and what they decide to do about it.

## GM Toolkit: Reaction Tables

**When PCs push Cordham with evidence:**

| What they have | How Cordham responds |
|---|---|
| Log discrepancy only | Admits weight variance, blames road loss and Caltworks short-shipping |
| Mixed batch ledger entry | Admits supplementary sourcing, frames as proactive management |
| Silthen arrangement details | Acknowledges it, presents it as the solution, not the problem |
| Dessa Vael intermediary | Denies direct involvement, says an associate acted without full instruction |
| All of the above | Makes the full offer: retroactive compensation, transparent transition, contract reform |

**When PCs push Burl:**

| Pressure applied | Burl's response |
|---|---|
| Money | Refuses, Cordham pays better |
| Threat | Stiffens, becomes uncooperative |
| Knowledge of his debts | Shuts down, asks them to leave |
| Offer of help with the debts | Opens up, tells them about the Silthen arrangement and the blending |
| Evidence that Cordham is going down | Weighs his options for a long moment, tells them everything |
