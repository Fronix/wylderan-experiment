# Fenwick

> [!info] At a Glance
> A farming settlement on the bottomland between [[Pellan]] and [[Brindleford]], sitting on the best agricultural soil in the region. Fenwick families have worked the same strips for three and four generations. They measure wealth in stored grain and they are, for the first time in memory, running short.

## Origin

The bottomland here floods reliably, drains cleanly, and grows grain with less work than anywhere else within a day's travel. The first families arrived before [[Pellan]] had its charter and before anyone needed a name for the region. They stayed because the land was good. They built because they intended to stay. The settlement grew the way most farming places grow: gradually, without anyone deciding to build a town, until there was one.

## Geography & Layout

Flat river bottomland. Rich dark soil. Fields run in long strips from the river to the tree line, most of them worked the same way they were originally broken. The settlement itself is a single road with farmsteads along it, a communal grain store at the midpoint, and a small market yard where buyers from [[Pellan]] come on collecting days.

The road south to [[Pellan]] takes half a day by foot, less by cart. The road north through [[Brindleford]] is the secondary route, longer but sometimes more useful when Pellan buyers are offering poor prices.

## People & Culture

Most families here have three or four generations on the same land. They are conservative, skeptical of outside interest, and deeply practical. Social standing comes from yield and reliability, not from coin or connection. A family that stored its grain well through a bad season and helped a neighbor is more trusted than a family that sold early and came out ahead.

Two poor rain seasons have changed the mood. Families that have always been comfortable are quietly anxious. The conversation at the communal grain store has shifted from yield comparisons to questions about who is holding back and who is selling.

## Economy

Wheat and barley on the better-drained plots, turnips and parsnips on the lower ones. Fenwick feeds [[Pellan]] and, in good years, has surplus going north through [[Brindleford]] to [[Thornmeet]].

The primary arrangement is direct sale to Pellan's mills, which means selling at prices the miller's guild sets. Farmers who want to sell elsewhere can take the northern road, but the trip costs time and the market there is less reliable. Most Fenwick grain goes south.

Two bad harvests have created pressure. Families are holding back reserves rather than selling at market. This is rational individually and collectively it is driving up bread prices in [[Pellan]] and [[Harren's Gate]]. The miller's guild is demanding Fenwick release grain at last season's prices. Fenwick farmers are refusing.

## Tensions

The rain shortfall is the immediate problem, but the water rights question underneath it is older and has not been resolved. The irrigation channels from the eastern tributary are communally maintained. Three upstream families have been diverting extra water to their plots for at least two seasons. The downstream families are aware of this and have said nothing at a communal meeting yet, because saying it out loud makes it a dispute, and nobody wants to be the one who started a dispute with a neighbor they have to work beside every season.

The miller's guild's pressure from [[Pellan]] is escalating. The guild wants guaranteed volumes at fixed prices, backed by an agreement the communal meeting would have to ratify. Several Fenwick farmers think signing anything with the guild is a mistake. Several others are anxious enough about their finances that a guaranteed sale price sounds better than it should.

The Errick family, the oldest farming family in the settlement, has been approached by outside buyers about selling a portion of their bottomland for warehouse construction. The approach came through an intermediary, not directly. The Erricks have not told the communal meeting.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Errick land deal is further along than Gand Errick has admitted, even to himself. The outside consortium has made a specific offer for the eastern parcel, the one bordering the irrigation channel. If the consortium acquires it and builds grain storage there, they would have a facility between [[Pellan]] and [[Harren's Gate]] capable of buying cheap after harvest and selling dear in winter. This breaks the miller's guild monopoly but replaces it with something with less community accountability and no local loyalties.
>
> The water rights dispute is approaching the point where a bad enough downstream yield will force it into the open. If that happens in the same season as the guild contract dispute, the communal meeting will be asked to handle two structural disagreements simultaneously. Fenwick has no formal governance mechanism for either.
>
> Sera Veth's knowledge of what every family holds in storage is more detailed than anyone has asked about. She knows which families are genuinely short, which are holding back, and which are lying about both. She has not been approached by [[Pellan]] buyers. She would be useful to them if they thought to ask.
>
> The Errick land sale, if it completes, will be the first time a Fenwick family has sold to an outside party. The precedent matters more than the parcel. Every other family with debt pressure will see that the option exists.
