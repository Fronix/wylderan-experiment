# Kettle

> [!info] At a Glance
> A hilltop settlement in the coastal hills between [[Silthen]] and [[Galden Reach]], sitting on the rim of a bowl-shaped valley. About a hundred and twenty people, split between charcoal burners working the scrub forest below and goatherds working the rocky slopes above. The burn smoke is visible from miles in every direction. The underlying resource problem is not yet visible to anyone who has not been counting.

## Origin

Charcoal burners came first, following the scrub-oak and hornbeam that grows well on the coastal hill faces. The bowl valley gave them shelter and a single-track connection to the estuary road that links [[Silthen]] to [[Galden Reach]]. A goatherd arrived a season later with a flock that did well on the rocky upper slopes where nothing else could be raised.

More families came after both of them. The work was comprehensible, the fuel and meat were needed, and the location between two larger settlements meant a carter came through regularly enough to make selling practical. Nobody owns the hillside. The scrub is common land, the rocky faces are common land, and the bowl itself is where people built houses because it was sheltered and nobody had claimed it yet.

The name came from the shape. Smoke pools in the bowl on still mornings. The people who gave it the name were carters passing by.

## Geography & Layout

The bowl sits in the coastal hills, surrounded on three sides by higher ground. The settlement occupies the rim, with the charcoal burn pits scattered through the scrub forest below the rim to the south and east. The goat runs occupy the north and west faces, too rocky and thin-soiled for trees.

A single track leads down from the rim to connect with the estuary road below. The track is passable by cart in dry weather and difficult in wet. The staging area at the bottom, a widened flat section of the track, is where finished charcoal is loaded and goat products change hands.

On clear days the sea is visible to the west from the rim's high point. Most residents do not remark on this. They know what the weather looks like when it comes in from that direction and that is more useful than the view.

## People & Culture

Charcoal burning is slow knowledge. The burn technique, moisture content, pit construction, timing, stacking method, the difference between a good coal and a crumbled one, takes years to learn correctly. Families pass it. Newcomers can help with physical labor for a season or two but cannot run their own pit without guidance that the established families give slowly and conditionally.

Goat families have less of this insularity. The herd knowledge matters but goats are knowable faster than burn chemistry. The goat families have absorbed more newcomers than the charcoal families have, which means their households are slightly more varied and slightly less tightly bound to each other.

The two groups come together for market days when the carters come through, and for disputes, which are also market days in a sense. There is no formal authority. Arguments are resolved by whoever is present and willing to stop them, which is usually someone who has been there the longest and has the most to lose from the argument continuing.

## Economy

Charcoal is the primary export. The scrub oak and hornbeam burns hot and dense. Buyers include:

- [[Caltworks]], which uses charcoal for salt rendering alongside reed bundles from [[Silthen]]
- [[Rackby]], whose smokehouses run on charcoal through the curing season
- [[Galden Reach]], where the ship repair operations need charcoal for smithing and tar-making
- [[Silthen]], which buys smaller amounts for heating and the occasional burn at the reed sorting house

Loads go out by cart. The carters who run the estuary road take what they can carry and adjust routes based on who is buying at what price that week.

Goat products, meat, cheese, and hides, sell through the same cart network. The cheese keeps well enough for the Silthen and Caltworks run. Hides go to the Galden Reach leather workers.

## Tensions

The scrub forest is thinning.

Nobody owns the hillside, which means nobody has an obligation to replant, and nobody has done so. Families cut the sections closest to their established pits first, then expanded outward. The boundaries between family burn areas were informal and are now contested because two areas have reached each other. The Verrik and Ondel families have been operating in overlapping sections for two seasons without resolving whose wood it is.

The goatherds want to expand their grazing area into sections that have been burned and are recovering. Reed grass and young scrub come in first after a burn, which goats graze eagerly. This prevents the woody regrowth that the charcoal families need. The goat families do not see why they should manage their herd around the charcoal families' planning horizon when nobody manages the charcoal families' cutting around the goat families' needs.

If charcoal output from the hill falls, every settlement that depends on it for fuel absorbs the shortfall. [[Caltworks]] has reed bundles from [[Silthen]] as partial backup. [[Rackby]] has limited alternatives for smokehouse fuel. [[Galden Reach]]'s Smith has no backup at all.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Essa Verrik has been experimenting with reed-char, charcoal produced from dried reed waste purchased from [[Silthen]]. Reed waste is abundant, consistently available, and cheap, because it is currently treated as disposal material after Silthen's bundling process. If reed-char burns as well as wood charcoal for smithing and salt work, it changes the Kettle operation significantly: the scarce hillside scrub is replaced by renewable reed supply, and whoever controls the reed-char process has a supply that the scrub commons cannot match.
>
> The Verrik family has not shared the experiments because if it works, they intend to have a head start on production before anyone else can replicate it. If it does not work, they have wasted a season. The experiments are three months in. Early results are mixed but not discouraging. One successful large burn would clarify it.
>
> [[Hava Denn]] at [[Silthen]] has noticed that reed waste is being purchased, which is unusual since nobody has ever wanted it. She has not connected this to Kettle or to the Verriks specifically. She knows only that a carter is picking up bales of sorted waste reed and taking them west. If she discovers what the Verriks are doing, her response depends on whether she sees it as an opportunity for Silthen (a new revenue stream from waste product) or a threat (external control over a Silthen product).
>
> If reed-char works and the Verrik family develops it, the commons dispute with Ondel becomes moot, the goatherd expansion becomes possible (no need to protect recovering scrub for burning), and the power balance in the settlement shifts sharply toward whichever family owns the new process. The scrub forest can begin recovering. The settlements that depend on Kettle charcoal may get more reliable supply at lower cost. The Verrik family would own the supply chain.
>
> The Ondel family does not know about the experiments. Davan Hrek does not know about the experiments. If either learns, the settlement meeting will be significant.
