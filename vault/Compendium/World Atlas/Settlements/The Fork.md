# The Fork

> [!info] At a Glance
> A staging yard at the point where the salt road from [[Caltworks]] branches toward the coast and toward a handful of inland farms. Three storage sheds, a carter's rest house, a water cistern, and about thirty people who decided it was easier to stay than to keep moving. Every merchant factor operating between the flats and the coast passes through here. So does every rumor.

## Origin

Nobody planned The Fork. The road branched here because the terrain made it convenient, and the first carter to pull over found flat ground and stayed the night. Someone else built a fire ring. Someone eventually put a roof over it.

The rest house has been rebuilt twice but has never stopped serving the same function: a place to sleep when you are between places. Permanent residents arrived the same way the first carter did, they stopped, they noticed something was needed, and they filled the need. [[Tallin Oss]] built the crate storage commission structure eight seasons ago and has kept logs since. Praven Holdt set up feed and water for animals four summers ago. The cistern predates both of them.

## Geography & Layout

The Fork sits where the cart road out of [[Caltworks]] splits, one branch continuing to the fishing coast, the other climbing northeast toward inland farms. The split is not dramatic: a slightly wider flat patch, a marker post with directions chalked in regularly, the yard buildings set back from the road on both sides.

The water cistern sits at the center point, fed by a seasonal runoff channel. It runs full in wet season and requires rationing in dry. The cistern is the reason everything else is here.

Four structures surround it:

- The rest house: twelve sleeping spots, a common room, a hearth
- The main storage shed: three locked bays, rented by commission
- The animal yard: feed rack, water trough, hitching rail for twenty rigs
- The factor house: two-story building where factors keep rooms by the month

## Economy and Function

The Fork does not extract or produce anything. It moves things between people who cannot meet at either end of the road.

Factors who buy salt from [[Caltworks]] haulers do not travel to the flats in person. They keep a room at the factor house and wait. Haulers arrive, negotiate here, and continue to the coast or turn back. The transaction happens at The Fork, away from the weighmaster's yard, away from the harbor, outside the eyeline of the families who want to control both ends.

Storage commission is the main income for the permanent residents. Praven Holdt handles animals and overnight rig storage. [[Tallin Oss]] manages crate storage, keeps the logs, and has never lost a crate. The rest house is run by whoever is currently married to the Oss family, a detail that has changed twice in the last decade.

Travelers pay per night, per animal, per crate. No credit. No exceptions.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Cedd Miralle left [[Caltworks]] after [[Maren Seedeck]] blocked one of his purchase transactions without explanation. He believes she was acting on behalf of the Calt family. He may be right. He keeps detailed records of everything that moves through The Fork and has been comparing them against [[Caltworks]]' declared output, which he obtained through a contact at the weighmaster's house. He has completed the comparison. The numbers do not reconcile. His settled approach: he will not report to anyone in the chain, but he will share his findings with any outside party who comes looking for the right reason. He is not acting out of principle alone, he wants the Calt family's books examined because he thinks the fraud runs in both directions. Anyone who relies on him as a source should know his motives are real and so is his evidence.
>
> Tallin Oss's crate logs are the most complete picture available of how much salt actually leaves [[Caltworks]] versus what the Calt family reports to buyers. Nobody in [[Caltworks]] knows the logs exist as a consolidated record. If [[Dessa Vael]] or the independent crews ever learned about them, the tithe dispute would sharpen considerably.
>
> Oss recently received a message from [[Vess Cordham]] instructing him to hold all logs pending a routine factor audit and not to release copies. Oss read the message, as he reads all messages before deciding whether to act on them. He then made a copy of the discrepancy entries, the three seasons where incoming weights diverge from Cordham's declared Rackby receipts, and stored it separately from the main ledger, in the locked chest at the rest house rather than the storage shed. He has not told anyone. He does not consider this defiance. He considers it the same caution he applies to everything: a clean record held somewhere that cannot be touched by a factor's audit.
>
> The northeast branch (toward inland farms) is how small, unrecorded loads move. Irregular timing, no paperwork. Tallin Oss has noticed the pattern. He has not recorded it. He prefers not to know the reason.
>
> The cistern requires maintenance every dry season. Both settlements assume the other is responsible. Neither has ever sent anyone.
