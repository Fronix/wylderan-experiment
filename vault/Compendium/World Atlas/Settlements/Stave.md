# Stave

> [!info] At a Glance
> A timber camp in the northern hills that has been operating long enough to outlast its original stands. The permanent crew knows the accessible timber is retreating uphill every season. The buyers who depend on it do not.

## Origin

The camp was established by a logger named Osse Rint, who brought a crew of seven up into the hills when the valley timber near the road was already spoken for. The hillside stands were not spoken for. He cut a clearing, built a long-house, and started hauling. That was nine years ago. Osse Rint died of a fever in the third year and his crew chief, a woman named Brena Talt, has run the camp since.

Nobody filed a claim. Nobody was asked to file one. The trees were there and the road was below them and that was sufficient.

## Geography & Layout

Stave sits on a cleared flat cut into a hillside in the northern hills, roughly a day's travel west of [[Coldwell]] along an informal track. The main camp is a long-house for permanent workers, a covered equipment shed, a log yard, and a rough sawpit used for dimensional cutting of mine supports.

The tree line is farther from the camp than it used to be. The nearest working stands are now a half-day uphill. Crews leave camp in early morning, cut through the day, and haul back by evening or overnight if the load is heavy. Three seasons ago they left camp and were at the cut inside an hour. The change is visible to anyone who has been here long enough.

Seasonal workers sleep in lean-to extensions off the long-house or in a second structure that was built two seasons ago when the permanent crew decided the volume of work required more hands than they could keep year-round.

## People & Work

The permanent crew of eighty includes cutters, haulers, sawpit workers, and support staff. Brena Talt runs the camp. She has been here since Osse Rint's crew arrived and is the only person who has tracked the full operational history.

Cutting is organized by team. Each team works a specific stand on rotation, and Brena assigns the rotation. She assigns it based on which stands are still viable, which means she has a detailed picture of the hillside timber that she does not share with the teams themselves, who see only their own rotation.

The sawpit produces mine-support timber cut to Coldwell's specifications: a standard length and width that the Wulch shaft crews rely on. This is the primary revenue. Secondary income is rough-cut construction timber sold to buyers on the road below.

Seasonal workers arrive in late dry season when haul conditions are best. Most are hired through word of mouth. Returnee rates have been declining: workers who try the camp once and find the haul distance worse than advertised do not tend to come back, and Brena has been supplementing with first-season workers who do not know what the distance used to be.

## Economy

Two buyers underpin the camp's revenue.

**[[Coldwell]] mine supports** are the larger and more reliable contract. Orrel Wulch sends an order at the start of each season specifying lengths and volume. Brena holds the mine-support work back for her permanent crew, who know the cutting specifications, and charges a premium for the consistent sizing. The arrangement has been stable for five years.

**Construction timber** is sold to factors on the road between the hills and the salt flats. Volume is irregular and pricing is negotiated per load. This market requires less precision work but is slower to move, and Brena maintains a log yard for unsold stock that is currently larger than she would prefer.

The camp does not have an arrangement with [[Caltworks]] for firewood, though Caltworks uses significant wood volume for brine-boiling. Brena has been told that Caltworks sources its firewood from a closer stand to the east. She has not verified this.

## Tensions

The core tension at Stave is the retreat of the working stands. The camp is physically productive, but each season the distance between the camp and viable timber increases, which increases haul time, which increases per-load cost. Brena has absorbed this by taking on more seasonal workers, which reduces the per-load labor cost but increases the total number of people she has to feed and house.

The buyers do not know the distance is increasing. The mine-support pricing that [[Coldwell]] pays was set five years ago and has not been renegotiated. Brena has considered raising it twice and has not done so because she is not certain Coldwell cannot find another supplier, and because she has no leverage in that conversation that she is willing to reveal.

The seasonal worker attrition problem is not visible to outside buyers, but it is accumulating. The camp's ability to hit volume commitments on time has depended on the permanent crew absorbing overruns. The permanent crew knows this and has been quietly negotiating with Brena for better terms, which she has deferred.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The working timber at Stave will become economically unviable in approximately four to six seasons at current extraction rates. Brena Talt knows this. The estimate is not dramatic: the hillside has timber, it is just too far uphill to haul profitably at current pricing. The breaking point is not depletion, it is cost-per-load exceeding what the market will bear. Brena has not told this to [[Coldwell]] or to any of the construction buyers on the road.
>
> The Coldwell mine-support arrangement is what prevents Brena from acting on what she knows. If she raises pricing, Orrel Wulch may search for alternatives, which he could find, slowly, at higher cost to him. If she says nothing, she buys more seasons of stable revenue. The risk is that the breaking point arrives before she has found a way out of the arrangement or a reason to disclose it honestly.
>
> Dovin Strake is not convinced by Brena's reassurance about the hillside. He has not done his own formal estimate, but he has worked timber long enough to read a stand. If a PC asks him directly about the timber situation, he will be honest to the degree that he can be without contradicting Brena publicly. He trusts her in most things and does not want to undermine her, but he will not lie to an outsider who is asking pointed questions.
>
> Fenn Ossel's interest in approaching Coldwell directly is not disloyalty, it is economic logic. If Stave closes or contracts, the sawpit becomes irrelevant. If he has a direct relationship with the Coldwell operation, he has options. He has not told Brena about this interest and would be embarrassed if she found out. Orrel Wulch at Coldwell does not know the Stave mine-support output depends on one person knowing the spec.
>
> The Caltworks firewood market is real and Brena's information about it is wrong. [[Caltworks]] does not have a close eastern stand. They have been sourcing firewood through carters who collect from wherever they can, at rising cost, and the inconsistency in wood quality has affected brine-boiling efficiency. If Brena knew this, the firewood market would be attractive. The information gap is mutual: Caltworks workers complain about the wood but no one has been tasked with finding a better source.
