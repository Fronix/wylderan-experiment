# Stave

> [!info] At a Glance
> A timber camp in the forest east of [[Coldwell]]. The mill is the only structure that matters, and it is owned by a factor in [[Harren's Gate]] who has never visited. The camp boss answers to someone she has never met, for equipment she keeps running herself, at margins she controls more carefully than her instructions allow.

## Origin

The mill was built by a [[Harren's Gate]] factor named Keln Prauth who wanted a timber supply closer to the northern road. He hired a camp boss, sent equipment north, and collected his cut from a distance. That was seven years ago. Keln Prauth is still the factor. He has sent replacement parts and updated price schedules. He has not come north.

The camp that grew around the mill was not planned. Seasonal workers who came back year after year built more permanent structures. The cook shed expanded. A second bunkhouse went up because the first could not hold peak-season crews. The clearing is larger than it needs to be for a seasonal operation. It has become a settlement by accumulation.

## Geography & Layout

Stave occupies a clearing in deep forest, with the mill at the center. Log roads radiate out from the clearing to active cutting areas. The nearest active cutting faces are now a half-day haul from the mill, the close timber having been worked out in the first years.

Permanent structures: the mill shed, a bunkhouse for the year-round crew, the cook shed, a tool storage building, and a covered log yard for staged timber. A second bunkhouse was added two seasons ago for peak-season overflow.

The track west to [[Coldwell]] is the most maintained route. A separate road runs south toward [[The Fork]] and eventually [[Caltworks]], used primarily for outgoing loads.

## People & Culture

The permanent crew is the camp boss, four year-round sawyers, and support staff. They maintain the mill and keep output moving through the off-season at reduced rate. Most decisions are operational: which cutting faces to work, which logs to grade, how to prioritize the saw schedule.

Seasonal workers arrive in late spring and fill the bunkhouses through autumn cutting. The crew is mostly young and mostly male. Repeat workers know the mill's rhythms and the camp boss's expectations. First-season workers learn quickly that opinions about how the mill should be run are not requested and not received well.

The relationship between permanent crew and the absent [[Harren's Gate]] factor is a low-level resentment that has never been resolved because resolution would require Keln Prauth to show up, and he does not show up. Equipment problems that require his authorization take weeks to fix. The mill saw broke last season. Prauth had to approve the replacement part, which came from [[Harren's Gate]]. Three weeks of reduced output. The permanent crew nearly walked. They did not, because there is no comparable camp nearby and the work pays.

## Economy

Timber output goes to four buyers:

- **[[The Fork]]**: Crate wood. Planked boards cut to specifications for the crate builders who work the staging yard. This is the highest-margin sale.
- **[[Caltworks]]**: Building timber and fuel wood. Volume is moderate and consistent.
- **[[Coldwell]]**: Mine shoring timber. Specific dimensions required for shaft support. Smaller volume, critical use.
- **Southern road factors**: Occasional large orders for construction projects in [[Brindleford]] and [[Harren's Gate]]. These orders take priority in the factor's price schedule.

Keln Prauth takes a percentage of all sales as equipment leasing and factor's fee. The permanent crew considers this percentage too large for someone who replaced a mill saw at three weeks' notice and has not otherwise appeared.

## Tensions

[[Coldwell]] needs mine shoring timber to reopen its collapsed shaft. The order has been in since last season. Camp boss Sera Voll has the timber and the capacity to deliver. The problem is that Keln Prauth has deprioritized the Coldwell order in favor of a southern construction contract that pays higher per unit. Voll is caught between a neighbor settlement that needs the timber urgently and a factor whose instructions she is required to follow.

The permanent sawyers have separately been discussing what a direct sales arrangement with [[Caltworks]] and [[The Fork]] would look like, bypassing Prauth's cut entirely. This is not yet organized beyond conversation, but it is louder after every equipment delay.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Sera Voll has been keeping two sets of books. One set goes to Keln Prauth in [[Harren's Gate]], showing standard output and costs. The second set is her own, showing actual output, which runs roughly twenty percent higher than what she reports to Prauth. She has been selling the surplus directly to [[The Fork]], transacting through [[Tallin Oss]], and splitting the extra revenue with the permanent sawyers as a supplement to their logged wages. The sawyers know they are receiving something beyond official pay, but Sera has told them it is a productivity arrangement she negotiated with the factor. It is not.
>
> Tallin Oss knows the full arrangement. He has the real transaction records at [[The Fork]] and has said nothing, which means Sera has a debt to his silence and he has another entry in his collection of useful information. If the discrepancy in the Fork's incoming timber logs were compared to Prauth's records, the gap would show immediately. Tallin has kept this consistent with his other ledger practices: accurate on his end, no commentary on what the numbers mean.
>
> If Prauth finds out, Sera loses the camp and likely faces a legal claim on back revenue. If the sawyers find out she has been taking a larger personal share than she described, she loses the crew's trust at exactly the point when she needs them most. The Coldwell shoring delay has made her position worse: she cannot release the Coldwell timber without Prauth's authorization, but the reason she cannot advocate harder for it is that she does not want Prauth looking at her operation closely right now.
>
> The permanent sawyers' direct-sales conversation is not yet coordinated, but Fenn, the senior sawyer, has spoken to a [[The Fork]] crate factor informally about what a direct arrangement might look like. He does not know about Sera's books. If he did, his complaint about Prauth's cut would expand to include Sera's cut, and the outcome of that conversation is difficult to predict.
