# Silthen

> [!info] At a Glance
> A reed-cutting settlement at the mouth of the Silt Run, three to four days west of [[Caltworks]]. About seventy people, most organized around the reed plots lining the estuary's slow channels. They cut, bundle, and sell. At the seaward end of the estuary, where the river meets the tidal flats, a few families also work small salt pans, scraping crusts from the exposed mud when the tide pulls back. It is not their main business. A merchant factor named [[Vess Cordham]] has lately made it interesting.

## Origin

The first cutters came from inland, following a rumor that estuary reed burned cleaner and lasted longer than marsh grass. That rumor was correct. A roofing crew bought the first load en route to the salt flats, and that sale was good enough that a cutter stayed the winter.

By the third year there were six families. The work organized itself around the reed calendar: when to cut, when to let plots rest, when to burn. The salt pans came later, started by one family who noticed the tidal flats left crusts behind in dry season and decided scraping them was easier than another day in the reed channels. Salt was never the point. For most of Silthen's life, it has been a secondary income for one corner of the settlement.

That may be changing.

## Geography & Layout

The Silt Run is a slow estuary channel that opens into tidal mud flats before reaching the coast. Reed beds grow densest along the calmer interior channels, where silt deposits and standing water give them what they need. The main plots run about a mile along two parallel channels. Smaller secondary plots extend south along a third channel that narrows seasonally.

The settlement sits on a raised bank between the first and second channels, dry except in the worst winter floods. Buildings are modest: a bundle shed, a sorting house, a smokehouse shared between three families, a row of worker houses built close together against the wind off the flats.

At the western end of the bank, where the ground drops toward the tidal zone, three families maintain a small cluster of salt pans, raked level and bordered with low clay ridges. The pans are visible from the main track but nobody in Silthen treats them as significant. They have not been significant, until recently.

## People & Culture

Most residents arrived as cutters and stayed. Turnover is low because the work is specific. Reading the plots, timing the burns, knowing which sections need rest and which are ready, takes two seasons to learn badly and more to learn well. Someone new cannot do this work well for a long time.

The result is a community of long acquaintance, with the social patterns that follow. Old debts are remembered. Old favors too. Arguments tend to be specific and long-running, because the people having them will see each other tomorrow.

[[Hava Denn]] holds the burn schedule, which she inherited from the previous plot manager over seven years of apprenticeship. Nobody else has the full picture of which plots burn when and why. This is not an arrangement anyone designed. It is how the knowledge moved.

The salt pan families, the Voel family primarily, have always been a bit separate. Reed work and pan work share a shoreline but not much else. Pan work is slower, dirtier, and more dependent on weather. Reed work pays better in a good season. Pan work pays more consistently in a bad one.

## Economy

Silthen produces two things at scale: cut reed for thatch and dried reed bundles for fuel.

Thatch is the primary product. Cut in late summer, bundled by size and grade, sold to buyers who use it to roof working sheds, brine houses, and animal shelters. [[Caltworks]] is the largest regular buyer. Their brine sheds and sorting pavilions need re-thatching every few years, and Silthen's output fits the size of the job.

Fuel bundles are secondary but consistent. Reed burns fast and hot, useful for supplemental fire during salt boiling. Caltworks buys a portion most seasons. A carter named Kel Dusk handles most transport, carrying loads northeast along a track that connects to the main road toward the salt flats.

Salt from the tidal pans has always been small volume, low grade, and sold locally to whoever needed it. It is coarse, irregular, and not suitable for precision curing. Useful for preserving animal hides, salting roads in icy seasons, and rough pickling. Not the kind of salt a fish-curing operation would normally consider.

[[Vess Cordham]] visited four months ago. He spoke mostly with the Voel family. He has not been back but has sent a messenger twice since.

## Tensions

The plots are not recovering the way they used to.

Hava Denn has been tracking this for three years. Sections that needed one burn cycle now need two. The southern channel's third plot has barely regenerated after last autumn's cut. She has started skipping orders she could technically fill, choosing to let sections rest. She has not explained this to her buyers.

[[Caltworks]] placed a larger order this season than last, citing expansion of the outer brine sheds. Hava filled it. She pulled from two sections she had planned to rest. She knows this shortens their productive life. She made the sale anyway because losing the Caltworks contract would leave Silthen with no buyer large enough to absorb its full output.

The Cordham question is running alongside this. The Voel family has told the rest of the settlement that a Rackby factor has expressed interest in purchasing salt at scale, on a regular arrangement, at better prices than the local trade brings in. The settlement has not formally discussed what this means. Hava Denn has not been asked her opinion, because it is not her domain. But expanding salt production would mean expanding the tidal pan area, which means clearing reed plot margins, which means less of her plots, which means less of her work.

She has not said this to anyone. She is waiting to see if the Cordham arrangement materializes.

The Voel family is cautiously interested. They know their salt is coarse. They have not been told why Cordham wants it, or that he already has a supplier in [[Caltworks]] whose output is declining, or that he is positioning them as a replacement. They know only that a factor with money is paying attention to something they have mostly ignored.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> [[Vess Cordham]] visited Silthen four months ago to assess whether the Voel pans could supply enough salt to serve as a Rackby backup if [[Caltworks]] supply becomes untenable. His conclusion: not yet, but possibly within two seasons if the Voels expand production. He has not told Pell Voel why he wants scale. He has not told [[Rackby]] that he is here. He has not told [[Caltworks]] either.
>
> The Voel salt is genuinely lower quality than Caltworks product, suitable for rough preservation but not for fine fish curing. If Cordham tries to substitute it directly into the Rackby curing chain without upgrading the pans and refining process, the spoilage problem will continue. He knows this. His plan involves telling the Voels to upgrade their process before he announces the new supplier, and keeping the cost of that upgrade off his visible ledger.
>
> Pell Voel suspects there is more to the arrangement than Cordham has said. He does not know what. He is negotiating carefully, asking for payment terms and exclusivity clauses that a local salt seller has no reason to know about. Someone has advised him.
>
> Hava Denn is the obstacle Cordham does not know about. She does not interact with him directly. But she controls what land in Silthen is used for reed plots and what falls under her rotation plan. If the Voel family needs to expand the pan area into the southern margins, they will need Hava to release those sections from her rotation. She will not do this easily or quickly. She may not do it at all.
>
> The third plot may be silting over permanently, independent of overharvest. If so, Silthen loses about a quarter of its reed productive capacity within three to five seasons regardless of burn management. The economic pressure this creates on Hava Denn may actually push her toward accepting the salt expansion, since a shrinking reed economy needs a replacement income. Neither she nor anyone else in Silthen has connected these dots yet.
