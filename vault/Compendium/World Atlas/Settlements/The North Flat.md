# The North Flat

> [!info] At a Glance
> A secondary salt flat half a day north of [[Caltworks]], worked by three families who walked off the main flats two seasons ago and have not come back. No infrastructure, no weighmaster, no tithe. They built their own drying racks and they pack their own crates. Production is growing. The Calt family has not yet decided what to do about that.

## Origin

Three families, the Parets, the Dunholts, and the Kellith clan, left the main [[Caltworks]] flats two seasons ago after a dispute over the tithe calculation on a shared processing batch. The calculation was not an accident. They had been shortchanged before. This time they did not argue, they loaded their tools on two carts and followed the North Track until they found a flat that nobody had filed any kind of claim on, which was not difficult because nobody files claims on anything out here.

They set up that first season with borrowed equipment and sold their first crates directly to a carter heading for the coast. It was not profitable. The second season was better.

## Geography & Layout

The flat lies north of the main [[Caltworks]] works along the North Track, a half-day on foot and a few hours by cart. The track is serviceable in dry season. In wet season the last stretch softens badly enough that loads have to be split across two carts or left until it firms.

The flat itself is shallower and more irregular than the main [[Caltworks]] evaporation pans. The mineral crust forms in patches rather than sheets. Productive area is smaller but the families have learned which patches run richest and schedule their scraping around that. They lose less to low-yield sections than they lost to the tithe.

Current structures:

- Two long drying racks built from salvaged timber, covered with waxed canvas in wet season
- A shared tool shed, padlocked, key held by [[Senna Paret]]
- A fire pit and a rain cistern that holds maybe two weeks' water
- A single overnight shelter for workers staying through early-morning scraping shifts

There is no permanent residence at the flat. The families live at the settlement edge of [[Caltworks]], in the Outer Ring. They walk out in the morning and come back at night, or stay over when a scraping run requires it.

## People & Work

The three families divide labor by household rather than by task. Each family runs its own processing, uses its own crates, and hauls its own loads. They share the tool shed, the cistern, and the overnight shelter. They do not share revenue. This arrangement has held because there has been no reason to fight over it yet.

[[Senna Paret]] is the oldest head among the three and the one who called the meeting two days ago. She controls the key to the tool shed. This is not a formal leadership role. It functions as one.

The Dunholt family has a son, Bram Dunholt, still working the morning weighing queue at the main flats. He has not left with them. This is either loyalty to the main settlement or a hedge. Nobody has asked him directly.

The Kellith clan is the youngest operation. Fen Kellith arrived in [[Caltworks]] four years ago and was never comfortable with the tithe arrangement. He is the most willing to consider selling or moving on.

## Economy

The north flat sells crates through two channels: a carter contact who hauls to the coast road, and occasional direct sales to factors passing through [[The Fork]]. Neither channel is reliable. They do not have an arrangement with [[Vess Cordham]] and have not approached him.

The absence of a tithe means net revenue per crate is higher than it was on the main flats. The absence of infrastructure means handling costs are also higher. Total margin is probably comparable to what they earned before, possibly slightly better. The Parets track their numbers. The Kellith clan does not.

They have no formal relationship with any buyer. Every sale is direct negotiation. This is slower and more uncertain, but nobody takes a cut.

## Tensions

The Calt family has noticed the north flat's growing production. The two options they have discussed are acquisition (buy out the families' informal claim, absorb them under the tithe) and undercutting (flood the road with Caltworks salt at a price the north flat cannot match). Neither option has been acted on. Bevan Calt's apparent exit has stalled the decision: Oran will not move on this without a clear family position, and [[Bevan Calt]] is not available to form one.

The three families know they are being watched. This is part of why [[Senna Paret]] called the meeting. The succession uncertainty changes the calculation: an unknown outside buyer who acquires the Calt claim might act faster and less predictably than the Calts themselves have.

The north flat families also know that their claim is as undocumented as the Calt claim. This is not something they discuss directly, but it is present in how carefully they have avoided any action that could be read as a formal challenge. A challenge requires a forum to challenge in, and nobody has identified one.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The north flat salt is better than the current [[Caltworks]] output. The main flats have been degrading for years. The north flat is two seasons old and working fresher crust. No one has formally compared the mineral content because no one has had the tools and the reason simultaneously, but a curer at [[Rackby]] who tested both would notice. If this becomes known, the north flat families go from a nuisance to an asset. [[Vess Cordham]], who has been quietly compensating for [[Caltworks]] degradation through [[Hava Denn]]'s grey salt at [[Silthen]], does not know the north flat exists as a supply option. If he learned about it, he would approach the families before the Calts could acquire them.
>
> The meeting [[Senna Paret]] called had one item: whether to approach a factor directly and establish a formal supply arrangement before the Calt claim transfers to an unknown buyer. She proposed it. Fen Kellith supports it. Bram Dunholt's family is split, because Bram still has his job at the main flats and a direct supply arrangement would read as a declaration of permanent separation. The meeting ended without a decision. They will meet again before the next scraping run.
>
> Bram Dunholt is not reporting on his family to the Calt family. He is, however, talking to [[Nael Calt]], who manages the outer flats and who has been careful not to antagonize the north flat families. Nael has not told Oran about these conversations. If the Calt claim transfers to an outside buyer and [[Nael Calt]] is left without formal standing, the north flat families are the most natural ally she has.
>
> The Calt acquisition option is less viable than it appears. The Calts would need to offer something the families value more than the absence of a tithe. Cash is possible, but the families have no reason to trust a Calt-controlled crate valuation. Any offer structured around tithe credits or future weighmaster priority would be read as exactly what it is.
>
> The undercutting option has a ceiling. [[Caltworks]] cannot flood the road with cheap salt without signaling to [[Rackby]] and [[Vess Cordham]] that something is wrong with pricing, which would open exactly the questions about salt quality they are trying to suppress.
