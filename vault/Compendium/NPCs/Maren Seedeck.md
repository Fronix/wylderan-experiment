# Maren Seedeck

> [!info] At a Glance
> A woman in her forties who runs the weighing house like it is her own business, because functionally it is.

## Appearance

Stocky, with work-roughened hands and short dark hair she ties back with whatever is nearest. She sits at her scale table the same way other people sit at thrones. Her clothes are practical and salt-stained. She has a slight squint from years of reading tallies in bad light.

## Personality

**First impression:** Efficient, a little terse, fair in an obvious way. She answers questions about weight and yield without any ceremony.

**Deeper impression:** She is the most informed person in [[Caltworks]] and she knows it. She does not use this to accumulate wealth. She uses it to stay indispensable, which she finds more reliable.

**What they hide:** She is quietly exhausted. She took this job because it needed doing and no one else would do it. Now that it matters, she cannot hand it off without the whole thing unraveling. She has no plan for what comes next.

## Backstory

Maren arrived in [[Caltworks]] six years ago with a trading caravan that kept moving. She had ledger skills, the caravan did not need them as much as the camp did, and she stayed.

Nobody appointed her Salt Warden. The [[Calt family]] asked her to weigh a shipment and she did it right. Then another hauler asked, then another. Within a year she had a shed and a scale and a queue every morning. The [[Calt family]] could have taken over the operation at any point in those early years. They did not, because sitting in a shed weighing salt all day looked like a bad deal. Now they cannot take it back without making an enemy of everyone who trusts her.

She has no stake in who owns the flats. She cares about the integrity of the ledger. But the ledger, and who gets to see it, is everything.

## What They Want

**Conscious goal:** To keep the weighing system honest and functioning.

**Deeper need:** To be allowed to hand this off to someone competent before it becomes a political office, which would ruin the thing she built.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign role:** The neutral party who cannot stay neutral forever. She is the first person any faction will try to claim or discredit as the [[Caltworks]] situation escalates.
>
> **Key secret:** She has been approached twice by outside merchants who want her to start a tiered weigh fee, which would effectively let them buy priority. She has refused both times but has not told anyone she was approached.
>
> **Arc trajectory:** If the hauler-Calt tension boils over, someone will try to replace her with someone controllable. She will resist this, but she has no allies positioned to help her.
>
> **Roleplay notes:**
> - Speech pattern: Direct statements, no hedging. She talks about numbers the way other people talk about the weather.
> - Mannerisms: Taps the scale frame when she is thinking. Keeps her hands busy.
> - Sample dialogue: "Fourteen crates at standard. Sign here. Next."
