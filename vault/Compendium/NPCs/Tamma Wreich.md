# Tamma Wreich

> [!info] At a Glance
> The largest boat owner on Greymere lake and the person whose agreement makes or breaks any council decision. She has run boats on this water for twelve years and has never needed anyone to explain the numbers to her twice.

## Appearance

A broad-shouldered woman in her late forties, comfortable in weather. She dresses practically: oiled wool, heavy boots, a short cloak she keeps folded over one arm indoors. Her hair is cut close on the sides and pulled back at the nape. She has a scar across her left hand from a line failure years ago and does not mention it.

She takes up room without trying. When she stands still, people tend to give her space. When she moves through the Landing, the gut-table workers track her without looking directly at her.

## Personality

**First impression:** Direct and economical. She asks one question at a time, listens to the full answer, then asks the next. She does not warm up the room before getting to the point. First-time conversations feel like interviews.

**Deeper impression:** She is running the same calculation she runs before every season: what is the risk, who holds it, and who will absorb the loss when things go wrong. The current situation is unusually clear to her. Three families are already losing. Cordham has an explanation for everything and she does not trust any of it. She hired outside people because everyone local is already in someone's arrangement.

**What they hide:** She has been considering going around Cordham entirely. She does not know who to contact at [[Caltworks]] and has not asked anyone, because asking creates an obligation she is not ready to offer. The idea is further along than she has let on.

## Backstory

Tamma Wreich grew up in Rackby, the second child of a boat owner who treated the family operation like a problem to be optimized. She learned to read contracts before she learned to navigate, which her father considered the right order. When he died, she had the debts paid off within two seasons by cutting three routes that were bleeding margins and replacing them with two that were not.

She now runs seven boats, the largest single fleet on the lake. Her agreement is required before any council decision holds, not by formal rule but because everyone learned early that decisions made without her tended to fail. She does not chair the council. She attends it and says what she thinks, and people do the math.

[[Vess Cordham]] has managed the salt arrangement for eleven years. For most of that time, Wreich considered him reliable, difficult in negotiations but not dishonest. The spoilage problem has changed her assessment. She is not sure yet whether she is looking at negligence or something more deliberate, and that uncertainty is the thing she is most unwilling to show.

## What They Want

**Conscious goal:** To understand whether the spoilage is Cordham's error or Cordham's choice, and to have enough information to sit across from him and not be managed.

**Deeper need:** She wants Rackby's supply chain to be legible to her. Eleven years of Cordham being the sole point of contact between this settlement and its salt has produced a situation where she cannot assess the risk herself. She intends to change that, but has not yet decided at whose expense.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign role:** The accountability mechanism at the Rackby end. She is the person the PCs report to and the person who will drive any confrontation with Cordham. What she is willing to do depends entirely on what the PCs give her to work with.
>
> **Key secret:** She has been quietly calculating what it would take to establish a direct line to [[Caltworks]], bypassing Cordham on the salt side. If the PCs connect her to [[Maren Seedeck]], she will move on it faster than anyone expects. She does not want Cordham destroyed, she wants an arrangement where she is not dependent on his honesty.
>
> **Arc trajectory:** If the PCs bring her solid evidence, she moves quickly and negotiates hard. If they bring her partial evidence, she is careful and protects herself by framing the confrontation as a quality dispute rather than fraud. If they bring her nothing usable, she sits on what she suspects and the season ends badly.
>
> **Roleplay notes:**
> - Speech pattern: Short declarative sentences, no hedging. She states what she knows, what she wants, and what she will pay. She does not make promises she has not calculated.
> - Mannerisms: Keeps her hands flat on the table. Does not lean. When she is working through a decision, she goes quiet for a long beat and does not fill it.
> - Sample dialogue: "We lost close to two hundred barrels last year. We are on track to lose more this year and the season is not half over. Three families are looking at a year of loss. I want to know where the fault lies before I sit down with Cordham and let him explain it to me."
