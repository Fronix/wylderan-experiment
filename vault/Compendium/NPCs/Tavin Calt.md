# Tavin Calt

> [!info] At a Glance
> Oran's eldest son, who has done the math on the flats and does not like what it says.

## Appearance

Mid-thirties, with Oran's height but a leaner build, the result of less physical labor and more time at ledgers. His hands are calloused but not the thick-grained kind that come from daily salt work. He dresses practically, good cloth but nothing that announces money. Usually has ink on his fingers. He listens more than he talks in groups, and when he does speak he tends to be brief.

## Personality

**First impression:** Polite, measured, asks reasonable questions. Easy to underestimate as someone just learning the family business.

**Deeper impression:** He has already made up his mind about several things and is waiting for the right moment to say them. He is not cold but he has learned to be patient with his father's certainty, which has given him the habit of letting others believe they are still persuading him.

**What they hide:** He has been corresponding with a factor in Osset, a mid-sized river town two weeks north, about acquiring a trading house stake. He has not committed. He has not told Oran. He is waiting to see if the flats situation resolves itself, but he does not believe it will.

## Backstory

Tavin grew up working the flats the same as every Calt child, but unlike his father he was also taught the ledgers. He learned to read the production logs early, and by his early twenties had noticed that each season's net yield, corrected for crew size, was slightly lower than the one before. He brought this to Oran once, carefully, and Oran told him the flats ran in cycles and this was a lean stretch. That was eight years ago.

He has watched the breakaway families establish themselves on the north flat and done the math on that too. If the north flat continues growing while the main flat continues declining, the Calt tithe becomes a claim on a second-rate operation. Whatever leverage the family still has comes from being first among active producers. That window is closing.

He is not unsentimental about the settlement. He remembers being proud of the family name as a boy. But pride is not a business plan, and he has seen what happens to families who hold on too long to things that are done.

## What They Want

**Conscious goal:** Preserve as much of the family's capital as possible, in a form that survives whether or not the flats continue producing.

**Deeper need:** Permission, from his father or from circumstances, to stop pretending the flats are not in decline. He is tired of the performance of certainty he has watched Oran give for a decade.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign role:** The person who will confirm, off the record, what nobody in the family will say in public. If the PCs earn his trust, he is the most useful source of honest information in Caltworks. He wants to believe the settlement can adapt, but he doesn't think it will.
>
> **Key secret:** He has a letter, not yet sent, formally inquiring about the Osset stake. It is sitting in his desk. He has been sitting with it for three weeks. If discovered, it would devastate Oran and confirm every fear the workers have about the family's commitment to the settlement.
>
> **Arc trajectory:** If nothing changes, he sells within two years and leaves, taking enough family capital with him to set up modestly elsewhere. If something shifts the situation (a real solution to the flats decline, a political change that doesn't require the Calts to keep performing permanence), he might stay. He is not gone yet.
>
> **Roleplay notes:**
> - Speech pattern: Economical. He does not repeat himself. He asks clarifying questions instead of making assumptions.
> - Mannerisms: Habit of glancing toward the flats when the conversation turns to the family's future, the same unconscious gesture as Oran, in the opposite emotional register.
> - Sample dialogue: "I'm not saying leave. I'm saying know what leave looks like, so you're not deciding in the middle of a crisis."
