# Hava Denn

> [!info] At a Glance
> The woman who decides which plots get worked and which grey salt goes to market. She has managed Silthen's communal reed plots for nine years and treats every outside arrangement as provisional until it proves itself.

## Appearance

A compact woman in her mid-forties, built for work in wet ground. Her clothes are practical and water-resistant, heavy wool treated with rendered fat that smells faintly of it year-round. Her hands are wide and calloused across the palms from pole work in shallow water. She keeps her grey-streaked hair tied back with a strip of reed cord, which she replaces each morning.

Her voice is quiet and she tends to pause before responding, a habit from years of managing disputes where the first thing said is rarely the whole of it.

## Personality

**First impression:** Methodical and fair. She listens more than she talks and keeps clear accounts of what she said, to whom, and when. Community members trust her with shared resources because she treats her own holdings the same way she treats the collective's.

**Deeper impression:** She is measuring Cordham's arrangement against a timeline she has not disclosed. His orders for grey salt have grown three seasons running, but his explanations for why have been thin and inconsistent. She has been increasing output quietly without putting it to the full collective, because she is not yet sure whether this buyer is an opportunity or a dependency taking shape.

**What they hide:** She has started asking questions through other channels about where Cordham's grey salt is actually going. She has not learned enough yet to be alarmed, but she is paying attention.

## Backstory

Silthen is a reed-cutting estuary settlement three to four days west of [[Caltworks]], built on ground where the river flats go brine-saturated in summer. The reeds are the primary product: cut, bundled, and sold for thatching and cordage. After each seasonal burn to clear the old growth, the ash left on the silt carries salt from the saturated ground. Someone started collecting it generations ago. It sells grey and coarse at a fraction of what mineral salt fetches, but it is a waste product that costs almost nothing to gather. The communal structure is older than living memory: shared plots prevent any one family from controlling the most productive cutting beds, and the scheme has held through two generations of drought and one flood that would have ended private holdings.

Hava Denn was raised working the plots and took over management nine years ago when the previous manager retired and nobody else wanted the accounting. She is not a leader in any elevated sense. She is organized, she is consistent, and she does not take more than her share. In Silthen, that is sufficient.

[[Vess Cordham]] found her four seasons ago, looking for a second supply source he could bring on quietly. He offered fair prices for the burn-ash grey salt and asked for discretion. She agreed to both. The quantities were small enough that discretion was easy: nobody in Silthen thought twice about selling off a waste byproduct. They are less small now, and the collective has started to notice the orders coming in.

## What They Want

**Conscious goal:** A long-term sale arrangement for grey salt that pays fairly without creating obligations she cannot meet.

**Deeper need:** She wants Silthen to stay self-directed. She has seen what happens to settlements that get good at supplying one buyer: they restructure around that buyer's needs and lose the ability to say no. She is trying to grow the arrangement carefully enough that this does not happen. She may already be too late.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign role:** The third node in Cordham's information structure. [[Caltworks]] supplies degrading salt, [[Rackby]] pays for it and suffers the spoilage, and Silthen is the quiet replacement being built in parallel. Hava Denn does not know she is the replacement. If she learns what Cordham is actually doing, her response will depend on framing: if she feels manipulated, she will withdraw. If she sees opportunity with new terms, she might negotiate.
>
> **Key secret:** She has been suppressing the full scale of the grey salt expansion from the collective. If they found out she has been quietly building Cordham's supply base without a collective vote, there would be a serious dispute about whether she overstepped her role as steward.
>
> **Arc trajectory:** If nothing changes, Cordham completes his transition and Silthen becomes structurally dependent on a buyer it never formally agreed to depend on. If Hava Denn discovers the full context, she either renegotiates hard or pulls back and the transition fails. If the collective finds out first, her position is at risk regardless.
>
> **Roleplay notes:**
> - Speech pattern: Short questions embedded in longer statements. She confirms what she heard before responding. She uses "meaning" as a check: "So the arrangement changes in spring, meaning before the thaw?"
> - Mannerisms: Keeps her hands busy. Tends to have a length of reed cord to work with when sitting. Does not stay still unless forced to.
> - Sample dialogue: "The numbers have been growing. I'm not saying that's a problem. I'm saying I'd like to understand what's driving it before I commit the next set of plots."
