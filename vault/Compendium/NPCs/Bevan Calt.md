# Bevan Calt

> [!info] At a Glance
> [[Oran Calt]]'s eldest son, heir to a claim he does not want.

## Appearance

Mid-thirties, with the Calt height but none of the weathering. He has spent the last few years spending more time traveling than working. His hands are not a salt worker's hands. He dresses better than anyone else in [[Caltworks]], which reads differently depending on who is looking at him.

## Personality

**First impression:** Polite and slightly evasive. He answers questions about the flats with the same vague authority as his father, but without the conviction behind it.

**Deeper impression:** He is not cruel and not stupid. He just does not want this. He grew up watching the flats work his father and grandfather into old age in exchange for a claim that everyone resents. He does not understand why anyone would choose that.

**What they hide:** He has already agreed terms in principle with at least one outside buyer. He is waiting for his father to become incapacitated enough that the sale cannot be blocked. He is not in a hurry, but he is not reconsidering.

## Backstory

Bevan grew up in [[Caltworks]] and left as soon as he had money from a good season. He has been back three times since, each time for shorter stays. He inherited his grandfather's share of the family claim on paper when his grandfather died. His father has his share. Nael, his younger sister, has nothing formal.

He has had two meetings outside [[Caltworks]] in recent months. One at [[Rackby]] with a factor he introduced as a "freight contact." The second location is unknown. He told his father the first meeting was about route pricing.

## Relationships

| Person/Group | Relationship | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| [[Oran Calt]] | Strained avoidance | He loves his father in the way of someone who has decided to disappoint him anyway |
| [[Nael Calt]] | Guilty distance | He knows she is the one keeping the flats running. He also knows his sale will cost her what she built. |
| [[Maren Seedeck]] | Irrelevant to his plans | The weighing yard is not part of the claim and not his concern |

## What They Want

**Conscious goal:** To sell the family claim before his father's declining health becomes a crisis he has to manage in person.

**Deeper need:** To be done with the obligation without being the person who destroyed something.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign role:** The heir who is the wrong person for every faction. Workers don't want him. The Calts need him. The buyer needs him patient and quiet.
>
> **Key secret:** The buyer he is negotiating with is not a local factor. It is a merchant consortium from outside the region, one that already controls salt supply in two other settlements and treats the tithe as a floor to raise. If this becomes known, the workers will treat any sale as a hostile acquisition.
>
> **Arc trajectory:** If nothing intervenes, he completes the sale within a year. If the workers organize before then, the value of the claim drops and he may be persuaded to negotiate differently. He can be reached, he is not ideologically committed to the sale, he just wants out of the obligation.
>
> **Roleplay notes:**
> - Speech pattern: Measured, slightly too smooth for the setting. He is used to talking to merchants.
> - Mannerisms: Never initiates conversations about the flats. Redirects them toward route logistics and trade terms.
> - Sample dialogue: "The claim is a family matter. I am not in a position to discuss it publicly."
