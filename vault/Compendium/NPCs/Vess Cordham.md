# Vess Cordham

> [!info] At a Glance
> The merchant factor who connects [[Caltworks]] salt to [[Rackby]] fish, and controls everything that moves between them. He has held this position long enough that both settlements treat him as a fixture rather than a choice.

## Appearance

A lean man in his mid-fifties with the look of someone who has spent decades on the road between places. His clothes are better than a carter's but worn in ways that show he actually travels. He keeps his hair short and his beard trimmed close. His hands are clean. He carries a ledger book in a water-resistant oilcloth wrap, always within arm's reach.

He smells faintly of woodsmoke and horse. His voice is even and measured, the kind that does not rise when he wants to make a point.

## Personality

**First impression:** Knowledgeable, practical, and easy to talk to. He has dealt with most problems before and has a reasonable explanation ready. He seems like the person who makes complicated things work.

**Deeper impression:** He operates through information asymmetry and has done so long enough that withholding is reflexive, not calculated. He does not lie outright. He simply tells each party exactly what they need to hear to keep the arrangement running, which is never the full picture.

**What they hide:** He has known for two seasons that the salt from [[Caltworks]] is degrading. He has not told [[Rackby]]. He has been quietly arranging a secondary supplier to the west, so that when the Caltworks supply becomes untenable he can step in as the solution to a problem he knew about and did not disclose.

## Backstory

Cordham arrived at [[Rackby]] eleven years ago with a single cart and a contact at [[Caltworks]] who owed him a favor. The settlement had no reliable salt arrangement then. Families bought from whoever passed through, at inconsistent prices and inconsistent quantities, and cured poorly in the lean years.

He spent the first two seasons at a loss, moving salt at a price low enough to make himself indispensable. By the third season he had the Factor's Store built and a room arrangement at [[The Fork]] to stage loads before the last leg. By the fifth, no other factor was bothering to compete for the Rackby salt run, because Cordham had the relationships at both ends.

He has genuinely made things work. The arrangement he built has been reliable for nearly a decade. He is not wrong when he says Rackby would struggle to replace what he provides. The problem is that he believes this so thoroughly it has become a reason not to examine what his position costs the people depending on it.

## What They Want

**Conscious goal:** To manage the salt quality transition smoothly, ideally by having the secondary supplier in place before [[Rackby]] experiences a season bad enough to force a formal reckoning.

**Deeper need:** To be seen as the person who solved the problem, not the person who knew about it and waited. He has been the indispensable factor for so long that the idea of being questioned is not just a business threat, it is a challenge to the story he tells about himself.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign role:** The slow-burning crisis made human. He is not a villain but he has been making small self-serving decisions for a decade and they are about to compound into a very large one. He is the natural center of any plot involving the salt trade.
>
> **Key secret:** He has had a secondary salt supplier arranged for four months. He is delaying disclosure until the transition is complete, so he can present it as a solution rather than admit he sat on knowledge of a problem. If [[Rackby]] learns he knew and said nothing, the relationship is likely unrecoverable. If [[Caltworks]] learns he is quietly replacing them, the Calt family will move to block or undercut the arrangement.
>
> **Arc trajectory:** If nothing intervenes, Cordham completes the supplier transition, frames it as a proactive upgrade, and survives the scandal at reduced trust. If the spoilage accounting happens before the transition is ready, he is exposed as someone who knew the salt was bad and kept charging as if it were good. At that point, both settlements have reasons to be done with him.
>
> **Roleplay notes:**
> - Speech pattern: Full sentences, never rushed. He gives context before conclusions. He asks questions that sound like curiosity but are fact-finding.
> - Mannerisms: Opens the ledger when he is buying time. Refills other people's cups before his own, a social habit that reads as generosity but keeps his hands busy.
> - Sample dialogue: "The Caltworks batches have been variable, I won't pretend otherwise. I've been looking into it. There may be a better arrangement coming. Let me make sure the details are solid before I bring you something half-finished."
