# Tallin Oss

> [!info] At a Glance
> The man who runs crate storage at [[The Fork]] and has kept every log for eight seasons. He knows more about the real salt trade than anyone outside the Calt family, and he would rather not.

## Appearance

A lean man in his late forties, weathered from years spent mostly outdoors. His clothes are practical and worn at the knees from crouching to check crate seals. He keeps a grease pencil tucked behind one ear at all times and a ledger book in the inside pocket of his coat. His movements are deliberate and quiet. He does not fidget.

His hands are ink-stained and careful. He handles crates the way some people handle documents: with attention to damage and sequence.

## Personality

**First impression:** Neutral, professional, and unhurried. He answers questions with the minimum necessary information and offers nothing extra. Factors and carters find him reliable because he does not gossip and does not have opinions about whose freight he is storing.

**Deeper impression:** He is uncomfortable with what he knows. Eight seasons of logs means he has watched patterns form that nobody asked him to track. He can tell you exactly how much salt has moved through [[The Fork]] in any given month, which days the loads were light, and which factors stopped using the road after a certain point. He did not set out to accumulate this picture. It accumulated anyway.

**What they hide:** He noticed the unrecorded loads moving up the northeast branch two seasons ago. He has not written them down. He has not told anyone. He prefers to keep the distinction clean between what is in his logs and what he has seen, and he handles that distinction by not looking too carefully at what he has seen.

## Backstory

Tallin Oss grew up on the salt road, the child of a carter who ran loads between [[Caltworks]] and the coast for fifteen years. When his father retired and the cart was sold, Tallin stayed at [[The Fork]] because the road felt like home and someone needed to manage the storage sheds properly. He built the commission structure himself: flat rate per crate per week, discounted for factors with regular volume, no questions asked about contents as long as the crate was sealed and labeled.

The logs started as basic commission tracking. Over time they became something more, a complete record of what moved, who declared it, how the weights compared to Caltworks' output figures. He did not intend to compile a document that could embarrass the Calt family or expose [[Vess Cordham]]'s arrangements. He just kept accurate records. The accuracy is the problem.

[[Vess Cordham]] sent a message two weeks before the current adventure period asking him to hold all logs pending a routine factor audit and not to release copies. Oss read the message, understood what it meant, and has not yet decided whether to comply.

## Relationships

## What They Want

**Conscious goal:** To keep running a clean operation. He wants to do his job well and not be the reason something goes wrong in a dispute that has nothing to do with him.

**Deeper need:** He wants to be left alone with his logs and his work. The increasing entanglement, Cordham's hold request, Miralle's interest, the unrecorded loads he has pretended not to see, feels like a series of decisions being made for him by other people's choices. He does not know how to name this feeling. He manages it by not thinking about it.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign role:** Oss holds the most complete factual record of Caltworks' actual output versus declared figures. His logs are the physical evidence that connects the salt degradation to Cordham's knowledge timeline. Without access to the logs, everything else is inference.
>
> **Key secret:** Cordham's hold request is sitting on his desk unanswered. He has not complied and has not refused. He is waiting to see what happens next, which is the closest Oss gets to a decision.
>
> **The northeast branch problem:** He has noticed irregular, unlabeled loads moving up the fork toward inland farms for two seasons. He deliberately did not log them. He does not know what they are. If the PCs ask directly, he will admit he noticed. He will not volunteer it.
>
> **Arc trajectory:** If the PCs come to him first, Oss is the neutral path to the logs, but access is not guaranteed. He will respond better to professional framing (factor audits, legitimate dispute processes) than to urgency or pressure. If Cordham reaches him before the PCs do, the logs will be locked behind the hold request. If Miralle reaches the PCs first, they arrive already knowing the logs matter, which makes Oss more guarded.
>
> **Roleplay notes:**
> - Speech pattern: Short declarative sentences. He does not ask rhetorical questions. When he does not want to answer something, he answers a related question instead.
> - Mannerisms: Touches the ledger in his coat pocket when he is uncertain. Moves crates when he is thinking through something, small adjustments that do not need making.
> - Sample dialogue: "The logs go back eight seasons. What specifically are you looking for?" (Pause.) "I can tell you if I have it."
