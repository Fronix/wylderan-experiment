# Cedd Miralle

> [!info] At a Glance
> A factor who moved from [[Caltworks]] to [[The Fork]] three years ago after [[Maren Seedeck]] blocked one of his purchase transactions without explanation. He has been building a case ever since. He has enough to be dangerous and not enough to be certain.

## Appearance

A man in his mid-thirties, compact and deliberate in how he moves. He dresses better than the road requires, a habit from his time working Caltworks where appearance in the weighmaster's house mattered. His clothes are not expensive but they are clean and pressed by a factor's standards. He carries a satchel with him everywhere, the kind with a reinforced bottom that holds ledgers flat.

He keeps his beard short and his hair neat. He looks like someone who decided not to let the road change him and is mostly succeeding.

## Personality

**First impression:** Precise and sociable. He knows how to make conversation in a professional context and does it well. Factors passing through [[The Fork]] find him useful for route intelligence. He knows who is moving what and when, because he pays attention and people talk to him.

**Deeper impression:** He has been running a private audit for three years. Every major freight movement through [[The Fork]] is in his ledger with a notation. He has compared his figures against the official output declarations from [[Caltworks]] using a contact at the weighmaster's house. The discrepancy he found is either a rounding error or evidence that the Calt family has been misrepresenting output to buyers for at least two years. He does not yet know which.

**What they hide:** He wants the Calt family exposed. He is aware that this desire makes him less reliable as a source, and he is careful about how he frames his conclusions. He has not figured out how to present his evidence in a way that looks disinterested. He is not disinterested.

## Backstory

Cedd Miralle worked as a mid-level factor in [[Caltworks]] for six years, handling purchase agreements for inland buyers who did not want to deal directly with the Calt family. He was good at it. He understood contract terms, he read the weighmaster's records carefully, and he kept his clients informed when conditions changed.

Three years ago he submitted a purchase agreement for a bulk lot of refined salt on behalf of a buyer in the eastern settlements. [[Maren Seedeck]] rejected the transaction without explanation. He appealed through the standard process. The appeal was denied. He requested a reason three times in writing and received the same form response each time: transaction declined per discretion of the weighmaster's office.

He believes he was frozen out on behalf of the Calt family, who wanted that lot for a different buyer at a higher price. He may be right. He relocated to [[The Fork]] partly because he had no future in [[Caltworks]] after the dispute and partly because [[The Fork]] sits at the intersection of every load that leaves the salt pans. If he was going to understand what the Calt family was doing, he could do it from here.

He has been comparing freight log estimates, road timing, and the official output figures for two and a half years. The numbers do not line up.

## What They Want

**Conscious goal:** Evidence that the Calt family misrepresented output figures, presented in a form that a buyer's representative or an independent factor's guild would act on.

**Deeper need:** He wants a clear accounting of what happened to his transaction. He has told himself the larger investigation matters more, and it does matter. But the original grievance is still the engine. If the answer turns out to be that the Calt family had nothing to do with it and Seedeck blocked him for an unrelated reason, he will find this harder to accept than the outcome where he is correct and they are exposed.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign role:** Miralle is the character most willing to share information with the PCs and most likely to approach them first. He is a useful source and an unreliable interpreter. His evidence is real. His conclusions are colored.
>
> **Key secret:** His contact at the weighmaster's house is Seedeck's junior clerk, who has been feeding him information out of his own grievance with her management style. If this comes out, the credibility of his source collapses, because it looks like a coordinated information operation against Seedeck rather than independent corroboration.
>
> **The discrepancy:** His figures show Caltworks declaring roughly 15-20% more refined salt to buyers than the freight logs suggest is actually moving. He does not know if this is fraud, quality substitution, or a record-keeping gap. It is consistent with the salt degradation problem but he does not know about the degradation.
>
> **Arc trajectory:** If the PCs validate his evidence, he becomes a useful ally with a verified dataset. If they discover his source's identity, they must decide whether to tell him, which affects how he handles any confrontation with Seedeck. If Cordham hears that Miralle is talking to outside parties, Cordham will characterize him as a disgruntled former factor with an axe to grind, which is true, and will frame everything Miralle says as motivated reasoning.
>
> **Roleplay notes:**
> - Speech pattern: He presents information as a sequence of premises leading to a conclusion. He tends to end explanations with "which means" and then state the implication directly.
> - Mannerisms: Opens his satchel when he is making a point, even if he does not take anything out. The gesture says: I have documentation.
> - Sample dialogue: "The declared figures and the freight traffic don't agree. That's either an error in the records or something that looks like an error in the records. I've been checking for two and a half years. It's not an error."
