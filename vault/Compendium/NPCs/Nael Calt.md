# Nael Calt

> [!info] At a Glance
> The one who actually runs the outer flats. Has no formal say in what happens to them.

## Appearance

Late twenties, with the same weathered look as her father but none of his stoop. She works in the dirt and it shows. Short hair kept out of her face with a rag tied at the back. Salt-stained everything. She is the only Calt anyone sees at the flats on a regular basis.

## Personality

**First impression:** Terse, competent, not interested in introductions. She is always in the middle of something.

**Deeper impression:** She has built something real out of the operational chaos her father let accumulate. She knows where every crew works, who owes time on which flat, what the yield was last season down to the crate. She does not perform this knowledge. She just uses it.

**What they hide:** She is frightened that the thing she has built will be sold over her head and she will have no standing to object. She has thought about asking [[Maren Seedeck]] whether there is any formal process for claiming operational succession, but has not done it yet. She is not sure she wants to know the answer.

## Backstory

Nael grew up in [[Caltworks]] and never left. She started on the outer flats at fourteen, took over scheduling when the previous foreman broke his leg and her father did not organize a replacement quickly enough. She has been doing it since. Nobody appointed her. Nobody is paying her differently for it.

Her brother [[Bevan Calt]] is the heir. He is not here. She is. The workers know which one is running the flats. Whether that matters when the claim is sold is the question she is sitting with.

## What They Want

**Conscious goal:** To keep the outer flats running and the crew schedules stable through whatever is coming.

**Deeper need:** To have her work recognized as something that belongs to her, not just something she is doing until the men in the family decide otherwise.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign role:** The overlooked competent person who is the most workable path through the succession crisis, if anyone thinks to include her.
>
> **Key secret:** She has made a back-channel arrangement with two of the three families at the north flat: they share yield data with her and she gives them advance warning of any Calt family decisions that would affect the north flat. This is not formal alliance, just pragmatic information exchange. But if it came out, it would look like she was organizing against her own family.
>
> **Arc trajectory:** If the sale goes through and she is left with nothing, she has enough operational trust from the outer flat workers to organize a competing claim on the secondary flat. She would not frame it as rebellion, but that is what it would be.
>
> **Roleplay notes:**
> - Speech pattern: Short declaratives. Numbers and specifics over vague claims.
> - Mannerisms: Keeps a folded schedule sheet in her front pocket. Pulls it out when thinking.
> - Sample dialogue: "The east section ran short three days this week. That is a scheduling problem, not a yield problem. I can fix the schedule."
