# Senna Paret

> [!info] At a Glance
> The oldest of the three family heads who walked off the [[Caltworks]] flats two seasons ago and built something without asking permission. She holds the key to the shared tool shed. This is not a formal office. It works as one.

## Appearance

A woman in her mid-fifties, thick through the shoulders from decades of flat work. Her skin is salt-weathered and her hands are dry and cracked at the knuckles regardless of season. She keeps her grey hair tied back with a strip of oilcloth. She does not dress differently on different days.

Her most notable habit is stillness. She watches before she speaks, and when she speaks she is not warming up to the point.

## Personality

**First impression:** Deliberate and unsentimental. She gives no signal of whether she likes or dislikes the person she is talking to. She is not rude. She is measuring.

**Deeper impression:** She has been doing the math on the salt trade for a long time and has seen every version of the calculation. She left [[Caltworks]] not in anger but in arithmetic. The tithe did not add up, it had never added up, and the last dispute only confirmed she had been right to keep a separate ledger all along.

**What they hide:** She is more uncertain than she presents. Calling the meeting was the easy part. The question of what to do next has no clean answer, and she knows it. An arrangement with a factor trades one kind of dependency for another. No arrangement means they stay exposed to whatever the Calt succession produces. She called the meeting because she does not think she should make this choice alone.

## Backstory

Senna came to [[Caltworks]] in her thirties, already experienced on worked flats farther inland. She joined the main operation, did well, and after a decade was processing with one of the better yields on the shared pans. The tithe took more of that yield every year. She tracked it. She said so when it was politic to say so. When it stopped being politic, she stopped saying so and started planning.

The shared-batch dispute two seasons ago was not the cause of the departure. It was the occasion. The cause was eight years of records.

She organized the move. She identified the north stretch of the North Track, confirmed no one had made any kind of claim on the flat she found, and negotiated the use of two borrowed carts. The Dunholts and the Kellith clan came because she asked and because their own numbers agreed with hers.

She keeps the Paret ledgers up to date. She knows exactly what the north flat is producing, what it costs, and what it would need to sustain a formal supply arrangement.

## What They Want

**Conscious goal:** A stable supply arrangement with a factor, established before the Calt claim changes hands. She wants the north flat to be a settled fact that a new owner has to work around, not a loose end they can tie off.

**Deeper need:** She wants the work to mean something durable. She left a functioning operation and built a less certain one because the math was wrong. She does not want to end up in the same position again in five years with a different name over the weighmaster's door.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign role:** The decision-maker the PCs can tip. The meeting ended without a conclusion. If the PCs arrive before the next scraping run, they arrive at the moment the question is still open. She will not lie to them, but she will not trust them until she understands what they want.
>
> **Key intelligence she holds:** She knows the north flat salt is yielding well and consistently. She does not know it is higher quality than the main [[Caltworks]] output because she has had no formal comparison. She has noticed that a carter who moved some of her crates mentioned a [[Rackby]] curer was asking about sourcing. She filed this and has not acted on it yet.
>
> **Leverage she does not know she has:** If [[Vess Cordham]] learned the north flat exists and is producing clean salt, he would approach her before the Calt acquisition could close. She is the more valuable supplier. She is negotiating from a weaker position than reality supports, because she does not know about [[Caltworks]]' degradation or Cordham's pressure around it.
>
> **Arc trajectory:** Three paths. First: she reaches a factor first (Cordham or otherwise) and establishes a supply arrangement, the north flat becomes a formal competitor to [[Caltworks]] and the succession question becomes more complicated. Second: the Calt claim transfers to an outside buyer who moves faster than the Calts, and she has to decide whether to negotiate or resist. Third: Bram Dunholt's family pulls back, the three-family agreement fractures, and she is running a smaller and less defensible operation. The PCs can push toward any of these.
>
> **Roleplay notes:**
> - Speech pattern: Economical. She uses numbers when she has them. She does not dramatize. If she says something is a problem, it is a problem.
> - Mannerisms: Has a habit of looking at the flat itself when she is thinking, even when she cannot see it from where she is standing. When she disagrees with something, she is quiet for a beat before responding.
> - Sample dialogue: "Two seasons, three families, no tithe, no weighmaster. We are not ahead yet, but we are not behind. That is different from where we were." (Pause.) "What do you need to know and why."
