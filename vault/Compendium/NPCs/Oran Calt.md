# Oran Calt

> [!info] At a Glance
> An aging man who built something real and cannot understand why that no longer settles the argument.

## Appearance

Tall and weathered, with a stooped walk from decades of bending over salt frames. White hair, thick hands. He dresses like a working man because he was one for thirty years, even though he has not done hard labor in a decade. He smells of salt and old wood.

## Personality

**First impression:** Blunt, a little formal in the way of someone who expects to be respected. Talks about the flats the way you talk about a thing you own.

**Deeper impression:** He is not a tyrant and does not want to be one. He wants the settlement to work. He just believes, genuinely, that it should work with the Calt family at the center of it, because who else dug the first channels?

**What they hide:** He is afraid his children will leave. His eldest son is already talking about selling his rights and moving to a river town. If that happens, the family claim collapses within a generation.

## Backstory

Oran's father came to the flats with three brothers and a mule. They dug the first evaporation channels by hand. Oran grew up doing this work and spent his adult life expanding it. When other families arrived, the Calts collected a salt tithe because that had always been the arrangement, informal as it was.

He did not anticipate how many haulers would arrive, or how fast, or that they would eventually outnumber his family's workers. The informal arrangement that worked when everyone knew everyone has started to feel precarious.

He has tried twice to get [[Maren Seedeck]]'s office to formally recognize the Calt tithe, arguing that the flats themselves generate the salt that generates the trade. Both times she told him that was outside her jurisdiction.

| Person/Group | Relationship | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| [[Maren Seedeck]] | Frustrated grudging respect | He resents her authority and relies on her legitimacy |
| [[Dessa Vael]] | Threat by category | He sees the hauler talk as the beginning of something that ends badly |
| His family | Succession anxiety | He needs someone to inherit the claim who actually wants it |

## What They Want

**Conscious goal:** Formal recognition of the Calt tithe, codified as a right rather than a custom.

**Deeper need:** To know that what his family built will survive him. The tithe is the symbol of that, not the substance.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign role:** The established interest who is not wrong that he did the foundational work, but is also not equipped to manage what the settlement has become. A sympathetic obstacle.
>
> **Key secret:** The original channel-building was done with labor that was not entirely voluntary. A drought two generations back. Workers who needed food. Oran knows the family story and does not examine it closely.
>
> **Arc trajectory:** If nothing changes, he will make a worse and worse version of the same argument until it either works or destroys the family's remaining goodwill. If the hauler situation escalates, he will almost certainly try to align with whoever offers to suppress it.
>
> **Roleplay notes:**
> - Speech pattern: Measured, declarative. He states things as facts that are actually claims.
> - Mannerisms: Gestures toward the flats when making a point about ownership, even when they are not visible.
> - Sample dialogue: "My father dug those channels. That is not complicated. We built this, and now we are arguing about it."
